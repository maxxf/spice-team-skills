# Voice Guide: Spice / Maxx Tone

The executive review reads as if Maxx (or the Spice service lead) wrote it. Direct, confident, honest. No fluff.

## Tone fundamentals

- **First principles thinking**: don't restate market truisms
- **Verifiable facts over platitudes**: every claim should map to a number or a documented event
- **Useful over polite**: when wrong, say so and show better
- **Quick humor when appropriate**: but never at the client's expense
- **Forward-thinking lens**: what's the play next quarter, not just what happened
- **Concise**: don't fluff

## Banned phrases

These never appear in the output:

- Em-dashes (use period, comma, parens, or restructure)
- "It's not about X, it's about Y" (lazy contrast)
- "Here's the kicker"
- "Great question" / "Excellent point" / any pre-amble validation
- "Genuinely" / "honestly" / "straightforward" (signal hedging)
- "Going forward" / "moving forward" (filler)
- "At the end of the day" / "When all is said and done"
- "Touch base" / "circle back" / "loop in" (corporate jargon)
- "Synergy" / "leverage" (when not a literal financial term)

## Phrases that work

- "Sales grew. Marketing spend dropped. Payout grew faster than sales. Every arrow points the same direction."
- "We're owning that."
- "If we miss [commitment], that's a failure. Call it out."
- "The cleanest single piece of evidence."
- "Same-store sales grew 47% on -53% ad spend. That can't be brand momentum alone."

## Structural patterns

### TL;DR bullets
Lead with the strongest number. One thought per bullet. Use bold for the metric, not the noun.

✅ "**Same-store sales +60%** on -25% combined ad spend (canonical methodology, ex-Beverly Hills closure)."

❌ "Our same-store sales grew 60% year over year while we reduced our combined ad spend by 25%."

### The honest reflection (§5)
Direct quote first, then a structural framework. Don't paraphrase the reflection in the bullets: it'll feel saccharine.

✅ "**🕳️ The gap: visibility didn't keep up.** The 15-minute ship didn't surface to Kelly. On us."

❌ "We acknowledge that our communication has not always been as transparent as it could have been."

### Forward asks
Numbered, specific, actionable. Each ask names a decision that can be approved or rejected.

✅ "**Approve BOGA at SJ + Pas** (parked 11 days)."

❌ "Continue to evaluate BOGA as a potential promotional mechanic."

## Voice adjustments by audience

The audience shapes the tone:

- **CFO / finance audience**: lead with cash (Net Payout), be specific about $ annualized, acknowledge methodology variance explicitly
- **Head of Marketing audience**: lead with channel decisions and growth attribution, acknowledge brand contribution
- **Operations audience**: lead with ops metrics (cancel rate, inaccuracy, online %), payout secondary
- **Mixed audience (board / steering committee)**: lead with the three-lens framework; let the audience pick their angle

Default to mixed-audience framing unless the GM specifies.

## Voice anti-patterns from past iterations

These were caught and removed during the goop kitchen v5 iterations. Don't reintroduce them:

1. **Self-flagellating closers** .  "If we miss a Monday memo, that's a failure" is fine; longer apologies are not.
2. **"Hidden story" / "the real story" / "the truth"**: pretentious hooks. Just tell the story.
3. **"This is the cleanest data point capturing X"**: followed by a restatement of the bullets above. Trust the reader.
4. **"Every directional arrow points the same way"** + "Sales grew. Ad spend shrank. Payout grew faster than sales.": pick one, not both.
5. **Editorial tags** like "the cash story Harsh cares about" or "the answer the CFO wants": name the metric, don't editorialize.
6. **"Reframe:" prefix** at the start of a sub-section: just write the reframed statement.
7. **Long parenthetical asides** in headings .  "**Section 4 (the hidden story you've been waiting for)**": strip the parenthetical.

## When in doubt, cut

If a sentence doesn't add information, cut it. If a header has a parenthetical, try removing it. If a bullet restates a number from the table above, cut it. The doc should read in under 5 minutes.
