# The audit, explained in plain English

This is the operator's cheat sheet. Read it before running an audit. The "For the client" block at the bottom is paste-ready — drop it in the deliverable's cover note or say it on the call.

## The whole idea in one breath

The delivery platforms tell a restaurant "your ads drove $X in sales." That number is inflated — it counts customers who would have ordered anyway. This audit finds the part of the ad spend that bought **nothing it wasn't already going to get**, and tells the restaurant what to do with each location's budget.

## How it works — the five steps

1. **Find the natural experiments.** We scan six months of weekly spend per location and flag every week where spend *changed* — started, stopped, jumped 30%+, or got cut. Each change is a real-world experiment the restaurant already ran without meaning to.

2. **Ask "what would have happened anyway?"** For each change, we build a baseline of what sales *should* have done if nothing changed. We build it three ways and blend them:
   - **Comp stores** — other locations that did NOT change their spend that week (the strongest signal).
   - **Last year, same week** — the location's own seasonal history.
   - **The brand's overall trend** that month.

3. **Measure the gap.** Actual sales minus that baseline = the sales the spend *actually caused*. This is the number the platforms can't give you, because they don't know the counterfactual.

4. **Read the result two ways:**
   - **Cut spend and sales held?** The cut was free — that money was being wasted. (This is the Goop / Ahipoki story.)
   - **Added spend and sales didn't move?** That spend bought nothing.

5. **Route every location.** Based on its ROAS, its spend-as-%-of-sales, the cannibalization finding, and whether its organic share is rising, each location gets ONE call: **Concentrate** (under-invested, add spend) · **Hold** (paying back, leave it) · **Pull back** (saturated, trim to new-customer-only) · **Cut** (waste, stop it) · **Fix ops first** (the funnel is leaking — spend can't fix it).

## Why each location gets ONE clear call, not a tier label

We don't bucket locations into a proprietary taxonomy. The action falls straight out of the numbers, so a GM can defend any single recommendation by pointing at the four signals behind it: ROAS, spend %, the counterfactual, and the mix shift.

## The honesty rules (say these out loud — they build trust)

- **Every finding carries a confidence level** based on how many comparable stores we had to build the baseline. Low-confidence findings are suppressed, not shipped.
- **We never tell a client to spend more into broken operations.** If cancel rate or menu conversion is in the gutter, the call is "fix ops first" — spend just funds the leak. No other agency's report says that.
- **The headline number is a range, not a false-precision point.** "$429K–$643K" is honest; "$536,204" would be a lie about how exact this can be.
- **We don't claim credit for what the brand's own growth did.** The audit measures the spend decisions, not the whole business.

## What the operator pulls (and from where)

UE / DD / GH, six months, weekly, **by location**: spend, sales, organic-vs-paid split, and ops signals (cancel rate, menu conversion, ratings). The skill hands you the exact click-path for each file at intake. 3P only — no POS / 1P data.

**Ask at intake:** "Does this brand run paid spend anywhere besides UE/DD/GH — SkipTheDishes, ezCater, a catering channel?" If yes and the spend is material, we add that platform to the pull so the cannibalization number is complete. If it's a rounding error, we note it and move on.

## For the client (paste-ready)

> Every delivery platform reports "attributed sales" on your ad spend — but that number includes customers who'd have ordered without the ad. We looked at six months of your spend changes across your locations and measured what your marketing *actually caused* versus what you'd have earned anyway, using your own comparable stores and seasonal history as the baseline. The result is a per-location call — where to add budget, where to hold, and where you're paying for sales you already had. Every finding carries a confidence level, and we'll never tell you to spend into a location whose operations need fixing first.
