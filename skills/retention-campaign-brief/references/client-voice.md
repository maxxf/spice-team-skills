# Per-client voice + Spice voice rules

## Spice voice (applies to all clients unless overridden)

- No emdashes. Use periods or parentheses.
- No "it's not about X, it's about Y".
- No "here's the thing" or "here's the kicker".
- No "level up", "supercharge", "unlock", "elevate".
- No "in today's fast-paced world" or similar throat-clearing.
- No "delve", "leveraging", "robust", "seamless".
- Sentence case headings. Not Title Case. Not ALL CAPS.
- Mobile-first. Most opens happen on mobile. Short paragraphs.
- Be useful before being polite.
- Cite specifics. "$5 off your next bowl" beats "great savings".

## MBFS — My Big Fat Shawarma

**Tone**: Fun, bold, casual, playful. Detroit pride. Family-owned energy.

**Hero phrases (use sparingly, not every email)**:
- "Come Get Stuffed"
- "Big Eats"
- "Eat Big"
- "Meals so good and big you can eat them twice"

**What to lean into**: 100% Certified Halal, hand-chopped veggies, house-made sauces, build-your-own format, value per meal 3x average.

**What to avoid**: Overly formal language. Generic restaurant speak. "Delicious". "Fresh ingredients" without specifics. Corporate copy.

**Example subject lines that worked**:
- "You're officially part of something BIG" (welcome)
- "It's been too long, let's fix that" (winback)
- "Congrats! You leveled up to [Tier]" (loyalty — note the one allowed "leveled up")

**Sign-off voice**: First-person plural ("we"), warm, neighborhood-business energy. Not corporate.

## HealthNut

**Tone**: Healthy, energetic, LA-casual. Smart but not preachy. Owns wellness without being pious.

**What to lean into**: Genuinely healthy ingredients, the LA scene, regulars by name, the loyalty + rewards program.

**What to avoid**: Wellness platitudes. "Nourish your body". "Mindful eating". Anything that sounds like a yoga studio.

**Hero phrases**: TBD. Build with Cindy in next planning call. Until then, write neutral-warm.

**Performance baseline matters**: HealthNut opens at 66-72%. The list trusts the brand. Do not abuse that with weak offers or filler campaigns.

**Sign-off voice**: Warm, first-person, no exclamation point overload.

## Ahipoki

**Tone**: Fresh, fast, California. Bowl-forward. Loyalty-driven.

**What to lean into**: Build-your-own bowls, ingredient quality, loyalty rewards, the points economy.

**What to avoid**: Anything that sounds like a chain. Generic "lunch hero" copy. Heavy emoji usage. (One emoji per send max.)

**Hero language for loyalty / points**:
- "Your points are about to expire" — works
- "You're [X] points from your next reward" — works
- "[X] free bowls waiting for you" — works at high point balances

**Strategic note**: All Ahipoki copy gets reviewed by Daniel Ramirez before send. Always include "@daniel review pls" tag in the design brief.

**Mike Zimmerman is sharp**: He notices typos, weak data, and any whiff of templating. Brief tightly.

## Voice checking before return

After writing copy, run it through these questions. If you answer "yes" to any, rewrite:

- Does this read like AI wrote it?
- Are there hollow phrases? ("amazing", "incredible", "exciting", "fantastic")
- Are the verbs strong? ("we cut" beats "we offer cutting of")
- Would Maxx scroll past this?
- Is there a single concrete number or specific?
