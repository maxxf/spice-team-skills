# Copy + Layout Reference

Headline and layout options for the front side of a ratings flyer. Back is brand-only per spec. Keep it simple: one CTA, one credit number, one logo.

## Headlines (Front)

| Direction | $5 | $10 | $15 |
|-----------|-----|------|------|
| **Value-forward** (Ffresh style) | GET $5 OFF YOUR NEXT ORDER | GET $10 OFF YOUR NEXT ORDER | GET $15 OFF YOUR NEXT ORDER |
| **Unlock** (Everytable style) | UNLOCK $5 OFF | UNLOCK $10 OFF | UNLOCK $15 OFF |
| **Gratitude** | THANK YOU, HERE'S $5 OFF | THANKS FOR ORDERING: $10 ON US | $15 ON US |

Custom is fine if the client has strong brand voice (e.g., "we cooked. you rated. $5 on us."). Run custom past the Growth Manager.

## Supporting Lines (below headline)

| Platforms | Copy |
|-----------|------|
| UE only | When you rate & review us on Uber Eats |
| DD only | When you rate & review us on DoorDash |
| UE + DD | When you rate & review us on Uber Eats or DoorDash |
| All three | When you rate & review us on your favorite delivery app |

## Photo Treatment (Front)

| Option | When to pick it |
|--------|----------------|
| **Single hero** (Ffresh) | Small menu, one signature dish, limited photography |
| **Multi-item collage** (Everytable) | Broad menu, rich photo library, premium positioning |
| **Type-led, no photo** | Minimalist brand, cheap print run, credit is the whole story |
| **Full-bleed photo with overlay** | Premium feel, photo drives appetite, headline sits on top |

## Back Side Directions

Back is brand-only. Three options:

1. **Logo-centered on brand color**: cleanest, best for brands with a strong wordmark
2. **Tagline + logo**: adds a short brand line under the logo
3. **Full-bleed photo with logo overlay**: appetite driver, reinforces the product

## Fine Print (lives on back, bottom edge)

Default block:

> Limit one $X credit per customer. Credit auto-applied within 48 hours of review submission. Expires 30 days from issue. Not combinable with other promotions. Valid on [platform(s)] orders from [restaurant name] only.

Swap language if credit redemption is manual (customer emails screenshot) or if expiration differs.

## Reference Flyers (annotated)

### Ffresh Kitchen, $5, UE + DD

Cream background, brown type. Headline stacked across three lines at top. Supporting line in uppercase tracked sans below. Single food plate in bottom third. Small playful wordmark at base.

Works because: $5 is unmissable, palette feels like kitchen not ad, food photo signals reward, zero clutter.

### Everytable, $10, UE only

Dark teal wood-grain background. Massive `$10` as the visual anchor. Six+ SKUs scattered across the composition. White supporting line at bottom. Bracketed wordmark centered.

Works because: the number IS the design, product variety doubles as menu education, dark palette stands out from typical marketplace marketing.

## Universal Rules

1. Credit amount is the largest single element on the front
2. Platform clarity (name Uber Eats, DoorDash, or both). Never vague.
3. Logo on front and back. Front small, back hero.
4. Fine print goes on back
5. One CTA. Don't add "follow us on Instagram" or other asks.

## Anti-Patterns (push back if brief includes these)

- Multiple CTAs
- Fine print on the front
- Headline smaller than logo
- Vague credit ("a reward") instead of a dollar amount
- No photo and no bold type treatment (nothing anchors the eye)
