# Gold Standard Storefront Comparison

Compare prospect storefronts against best-in-class benchmarks to quantify gaps.

## Gold Standard Profile

A "gold standard" 3P storefront scores 90+ and exhibits:

### Hero Image (15/15)
- **Present**: Yes, renders on all devices
- **Quality**: Professional photography, sharp, well-lit
- **Content**: Signature dish with steam/action, or brand moment
- **Carousel**: Legible at 200px thumbnail, no text cutoff
- **Example**: Chipotle's burrito bowl mid-assembly, Sweetgreen's harvest bowl overhead

### Menu Images (25/25)
- **Coverage**: 95%+ items have photos
- **Quality**: Consistent lighting, angles, backgrounds
- **Priority Items**: 100% coverage on top 20% sellers
- **Consistency**: Same photographer/style across entire menu
- **Example**: Shake Shack—every burger, same angle, same background

### Menu Structure (20/20)
- **Categories**: ≤7 (optimal), max 9
- **Order**: Signatures/Bestsellers → Mains → Sides → Drinks → Desserts
- **Naming**: Clear, scannable (no "Chef's Special Creation #7")
- **Modifiers**: Every customizable item has size/add-on options
- **Bundles**: 2-3 combo meals visible in first scroll
- **Example**: Cava—6 categories, clear naming, bundles prominent

### Customer Sentiment (15/15)
- **Rating**: 4.7+
- **Velocity**: 100+ reviews/month
- **Themes**: 80%+ positive, clear brand strengths
- **Delivery Issues**: <5% of reviews mention delivery problems
- **Example**: Dig—4.8 rating, consistent freshness praise

### Promotions (10/10)
- **Active**: 2-3 strategic promos running
- **Visibility**: Appears in "Deals" rail and search badges
- **Strategy**: Mix of new user acquisition + threshold for AOV
- **Rotation**: Promos change weekly to avoid fatigue
- **Example**: Sweetgreen—$5 off $25, free delivery on $15+

### Competitive Position (15/15)
- **Pricing**: Premium justified by quality perception, or value leader
- **Differentiation**: Clear USP visible in storefront (dietary, sourcing, speed)
- **Ratings Gap**: 0.3+ above category average
- **Example**: True Food Kitchen—premium pricing, health positioning clear

## Comparison Methodology

### Gap Analysis Template

For each dimension, calculate:

```
Gap Score = Gold Standard Score - Prospect Score
Gap % = (Gap Score / Gold Standard Score) × 100
Priority = Gap % × Revenue Impact Weight
```

**Revenue Impact Weights:**
| Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Hero Image | 1.2 | 5-10% CTR impact |
| Menu Images | 1.3 | Direct conversion driver |
| Menu Structure | 1.1 | AOV and navigation |
| Customer Sentiment | 1.4 | Trust and ranking |
| Promotions | 1.0 | Visibility boost |
| Competitive Position | 1.0 | Market fit |

## Benchmark Comparisons by Segment

### Fast Casual (Target: 85+)
**Reference Brands:** Chipotle, Sweetgreen, Cava, Dig
- Hero: Signature bowl/burrito action shot
- Menu: ≤7 categories, build-your-own prominent
- Sentiment: 4.5+ rating, freshness themes
- Promos: $5-7 off thresholds, free delivery on $15+

### QSR (Target: 80+)
**Reference Brands:** Shake Shack, Five Guys, In-N-Out
- Hero: Signature item close-up (burger + fries)
- Menu: ≤6 categories, combos first
- Sentiment: 4.3+ rating, speed/consistency themes
- Promos: BOGO, free item with $X

### Casual Dining (Target: 75+)
**Reference Brands:** Cheesecake Factory, BJ's, Applebee's
- Hero: Varied plating showcase
- Menu: 8-12 categories (acceptable for breadth)
- Sentiment: 4.2+ rating, portion/value themes
- Promos: Family bundles, $X off $Y+

### Pizza (Target: 80+)
**Reference Brands:** &pizza, Blaze, MOD
- Hero: Pizza pull shot or overhead
- Menu: ≤6 categories, build-your-own + signatures
- Sentiment: 4.4+ rating, customization praise
- Promos: BOGO, large for medium price

### Coffee/Bakery (Target: 85+)
**Reference Brands:** Blue Bottle, La Colombe, Levain
- Hero: Signature drink or pastry beauty shot
- Menu: ≤5 categories, featured items first
- Sentiment: 4.6+ rating, quality themes
- Promos: Free pastry with coffee, loyalty hooks

1. Hero Image (0-15)
2. Menu Images (0-25, scaled to 15 for visual)
3. Menu Structure (0-20, scaled to 15)
4. Sentiment (0-15)
5. Promotions (0-10, scaled to 15)

**Interpretation:**
- Gaps closest to center = biggest opportunities
- Uneven shape = inconsistent execution
- Balanced smaller shape = uniform underperformance

## Actionable Gap Outputs

For each gap >20%, generate:

1. **Specific deficiency** (what's wrong)
2. **Gold standard example** (what good looks like)
3. **Fix action** (specific task)
4. **Estimated lift** (score improvement)
5. **Timeline** (when to fix)
6. **Owner** (Spice or client ops)

**Example Output:**

> **Gap: Hero Image (Current: 6/15, Gap: 60%)**
> 
> **Deficiency:** No hero image present; storefront defaults to generic food photo
> 
> **Gold Standard:** Sweetgreen's harvest bowl—overhead shot, colorful ingredients, natural lighting
> 
> **Fix:** Commission hero photo shoot focusing on signature item with steam/action element
> 
> **Estimated Lift:** +8 points (6 → 14), 5-10% CTR improvement
> 
> **Timeline:** Week 1 (rush if possible)
> 
> **Owner:** Spice Creative Team
