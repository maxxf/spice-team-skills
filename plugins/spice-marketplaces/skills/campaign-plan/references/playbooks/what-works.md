## I. Ratings & Organic Growth

### The Sunnyvale Flyer Play
**The single highest-ROI move in Spice history.**

- **Data**: Sunnyvale organic share sitting at 6%. UE conversion 12%. Weekly sales $35K. Ratings velocity was flatlined.
- **Action**: Inserted physical flyers in delivery bags asking for reviews. Simultaneously tightened offer targeting to new customers only (stopped subsidizing existing buyers). Reduced ad spend as organic compounded.
- **Result**: Weekly sales $35K → $55K (+57%). Organic share 6% → 54% (+800%). UE conversion 12% → 28% (+133%). ROAS 3.6x → 6.3x. Weekly ratings doubled from 22 to 54.

The mechanism: ratings velocity drives algorithmic visibility more than rating score alone. The flyer created a review flywheel. More reviews → higher placement → more organic orders → more bags with flyers → more reviews.

---

### SoMa Launch: Sunnyvale Playbook Applied from Day 1
**Proof the playbook is repeatable, not a one-off.**

- **Data**: New Goop location opening in SoMa. Previous launches had slow ramp periods.
- **Action**: Applied every Sunnyvale learning from day one — flyer program, new-customer-only offers, location-based campaigns (not keyword), retention-weighted spend.
- **Result**: 172% of sales target in the first 3 weeks. Best location launch in Goop history.

---

### Sherman Oaks: The Anti-Playbook (Cautionary)
**What happens when you buy demand instead of building it.**

- **Data**: Sherman Oaks hit target at $79K/week. Heavy promotional spend was driving volume.
- **Action**: Kept spending aggressively instead of transitioning to the organic-building playbook.
- **Result**: Organic share collapsed from 76% to 30%. The location became dependent on paid. When spend drove the number, the location looked healthy. Underneath, it was hollowing out.

The lesson: high spend without the ratings/organic flywheel creates a treadmill, not a business.

## II. Menu Optimization

### Goop San Jose & Pasadena Menu Overhaul
**Menu conversion is the gatekeeper. Below 20%, the algorithm won't surface you.**

- **Data**: San Jose converting at 14.6%, Pasadena at 19.4%. Fleet average was ~44%. Both locations had massive impressions (247K Pasadena, 181K SJ) — the eyeballs were there, the menu was losing them.
- **Action**: Category restructure, description rewrites, value-priced entry points, hero image A/B testing. Identified Teriyaki Bowl as the undisputed hero item ($78K combined revenue, 14% of total). In Pasadena, leaned into emerging growth items (Rotisserie Chicken +318%, Family Packs +160%).
- **Result**: Pasadena UE conversion 18% → 26% (+44%). DD conversion 30% → 34% (+13%). Projected ~$8K/week additional revenue from menu optimization alone. SJ target: 14.6% → 22%. Pasadena target: 19.4% → 28%.

Key insight: SJ is a DoorDash market (72% of orders), Pasadena is a UE market (64%). Platform mix dictates which menu to optimize first.

---

### Goop SJ Spend X Save Y — Calibration Learning (May 2026)
**The right offer mechanic, the wrong thresholds, gets you nothing. Calibration to AOV matters.**

- **Data**: SJ menu work had lifted conversion from 14.6% → ~25% but stalled. Spend X Save Y at conservative thresholds wasn't moving the next mile.
- **Action**: Lowered the Spend X Save Y thresholds to "aggressive" tier (third iteration — first two were too conservative). Ran simultaneously on UE + DD.
- **Result**: SJ conversion **25% → 33%** (+8pts). Drove SJ's **second-best sales week of the year** ($50K) and its highest UE/DD week of 2026. Pasadena rode along on the same campaign.

Mechanism: spend/save thresholds need to be calibrated to the location's AOV. If the "spend" threshold sits 30%+ above AOV, you only catch over-orderers. Pull it within 10-15% of AOV and the threshold buyer crosses into the redemption zone. That's where the conversion lift comes from — not the discount depth, the threshold position.

---

### Goop Friday Depth Tweak (May 2026)
**Weekend is where the auction is most fierce. Deeper promo on Friday alone wins it.**

- **Data**: SJ + Pasadena Live with Spend X Save Y at standard depth. Friday conversion lagged weekday average despite higher impressions.
- **Action**: Single-day deeper promo depth on Friday at SJ + Pasadena (UE + DD). Higher save threshold for the same spend, only Fri. Other six days unchanged.
- **Result**: **Pasadena's best delivery sales week of the year.** SJ also peaked. Pasadena UE conversion 34%. **Ratings velocity 38/week** (from 20-22 baseline) — driven by higher Friday volume seeding more reviews into the weekend.

Mechanism: Friday/weekend is when every restaurant ad-bids hardest. Going deeper that one day rather than spreading the budget all week takes share at peak competition without bleeding the rest of the week's margin. Pair with the Sunnyvale flyer to compound the ratings flywheel from the volume spike.

### Temaki: The Foundation Problem (Cautionary)
**You cannot spend your way past a broken menu.**

- **Data**: 10% menu conversion, stuck. Marketing spend was active.
- **Action**: Identified this as a foundation problem — no amount of ad spend fixes a menu that loses 9 out of 10 visitors.
- **Result**: Demonstrates the Spice rule: fix foundations (menu, photos, descriptions, pricing) before adding spend. The 20% conversion threshold is where algorithms start working for you instead of against you.

## III. Spend Allocation & Efficiency

### Goop Retention Reallocation
**Retention delivers 14x better ROAS than acquisition. We were leaving money on the table.**

- **Data**: Portfolio running a 79/21 acquisition/retention split. Acquisition was eating budget.
- **Action**: Shifted to 55/45 acquisition/retention split. Targeted lapsed and frequent customers with specific offers.
- **Result**: Retention delivered 14x better ROAS than acquisition. Frequent customers +89%. Sherman Oaks grew from 60 to 95 orders/day without increasing subsidy.

---

### UE Audience Segmentation Framework
**Not all ad dollars are equal. Waste ratios prove it.**

- **Data**: Mature locations spending broadly across all audience segments. No visibility into waste.
- **Action**: Built audience segmentation analysis — existing/lapsed vs. new customers, by location maturity tier.
- **Result**: 41% of mature location budget going to existing/lapsed at $2.40 CPA and 47x ROAS (these people were already buying). Waste ratios quantified: UNICORN tier wastes $4.18 per $1 acquired. GREEN wastes $3.55. YELLOW $1.76. RED $1.64.

The counterintuitive finding: $19 CPO at 78% payout beats $13 CPO at 74% payout. Cheaper acquisition isn't better if the platform takes a bigger cut.

---

### Goop Offer Cannibalization Fix
**Blanket promos were subsidizing people who'd buy anyway.**

- **Data**: Offers going to all customers indiscriminately. Mature locations seeing 80% of offer redemptions from existing customers.
- **Action**: Segmented campaigns. Tested offer structures for new customer acquisition specifically.
- **Result**: Best performing offer: "Spend $30, Save $9" — 61% new customer rate. Growth locations naturally perform better (36-38% new customers vs. 80% existing at mature locations). Stopped paying for conversions that were already happening.

---

### Location-Based vs. Keyword Campaigns
**31.5x ROAS vs. 2.4x. Not close.**

- **Data**: Running both location-based and keyword campaigns across portfolio.
- **Action**: Compared performance. Paused keyword spend entirely based on data.
- **Result**: Location-based campaigns: 31.5x ROAS. Keyword campaigns: 2.4x ROAS. All keyword spend was reallocated. This single decision improved portfolio ROAS from 4.2x to 5.4x over 8 weeks.

---

### Goop Meta Ads Incrementality Test
**Most of the Meta spend wasn't doing anything.**

- **Data**: $2,713/week Meta spend across 5 Goop locations. Assumed it was driving growth.
- **Action**: Pre/post analysis comparing menu views and sales lift per location. Isolated variables.
- **Result**: Only Sherman Oaks showed clear lift (+9% menu views, +9% sales). San Jose was down -22% sales and -36% UE menu views despite Meta running. Sunnyvale's growth was entirely attributable to the flyer program, not Meta. Recommended reallocating ~$300/week from SJ/Sunnyvale to Sherman Oaks — the only location where Meta was actually incremental.

**Confirmed durable (May 13, 2026):** Killed Meta paid social entirely at SJ + Pasadena. Through end of May, **no measurable demand drop.** $1,150/week reallocated to UE/DD promo budget (where it became the seed budget for the Friday-depth tweak and Spend X Save Y aggressive calibration). Lesson holds: Meta is incremental at a small subset of locations, not the default. Always test before spending.

## IV. Paid Acquisition

### Counter Service: From Zero to 6.74x ROAS
**Rebuilt the engine without increasing the budget.**

- **Data**: Feb–Jun, Meta ads driving 0 OLO (online ordering) purchases. All spend was going to 3PD link clicks with no conversion tracking.
- **Action**: Rebuilt Meta as a dual engine — OLO Purchase Optimization + 3PD low-cost clicks. Rebuilt the pixel from scratch. Tested creative formats aggressively.
- **Result**: 0 → 1,508 OLO purchases in 5 months. ROAS 0 → 6.74x (peak). $8,477 spend generating ~$37,700 OLO revenue. CPA ~$5.62. No budget increase. Top 2 creatives (Mushroom Melt + Smokeshow) drove 48% of all purchases at 6-7x ROAS.

Creative insight: IRL, handheld, melty food-porn photography crushes polished studio shots. The winning format looks like someone's about to take a bite, not like a menu photo.

### Counter Service: The $600 Test
**Hard proof that ads cause sales, not just correlate.**

- **Data**: Wanted to test causality, not just correlation, between ad spend and sales.
- **Action**: Cut $600 from weekly ad spend.
- **Result**: $2,000 sales drop. 3.3x multiplier effect. Directionally proves the ads aren't just riding organic momentum — they're creating incremental demand. This is the number to use in retention conversations.

### Dayglow ROAS Decay (Cautionary)
**Every campaign has a half-life. Plan for it.**

## V. Platform Strategy

**Platform selection is a margin decision, not a volume decision.**

- **Result**: DoorDash: 89% payout. Uber Eats: 78% payout. DoorDash organic growth +46%. DoorDash Sponsored Listings: 8.4x ROAS. DD projected to overtake UE in total payout dollars within 4-6 weeks. For clients on both platforms, DoorDash gets priority spend allocation.

## The Meta-Playbook: Spice's Operating Principles

These 7 rules emerged from the data above. They're not opinions — each one has at least two proof points.

1. **Fix the menu before spending on ads.** Below 20% conversion, the algorithm buries you. (Temaki, SJ, Pasadena)

2. **Ratings velocity > rating score.** A 4.3 gaining 50 reviews/week outranks a 4.7 gaining 5. The Sunnyvale flyer is the cheapest way to manufacture velocity. (Sunnyvale, SoMa)

3. **Location campaigns crush keyword campaigns.** 31.5x vs. 2.4x. Not debatable. (Goop portfolio)

4. **Retention spend delivers 14x better ROAS than acquisition.** Stop the 80/20 acquisition addiction; default split is 55/45. **Every offer specifies its target segment** (New / Existing / Lapsed / DashPass / All). Blanket promos subsidize buyers who'd convert anyway. (Goop reallocation, segment offer matrix)

5. **Every campaign decays. Plan the exit before you plan the entry.** Creative refresh by week 4-5. Spend-down built into every plan. (Dayglow, Sherman Oaks)

6. **DoorDash first for new clients.** 89% payout vs. 78% on UE. The math isn't close. (Portfolio-wide)

7. **Prove causality, not correlation.** The Counter Service $600 test is the template. Cut spend, measure the drop, quantify the multiplier. That's how you retain clients. (Counter Service, Goop incrementality)

8. **Make blocked items visible to the client, with day counts.** Once goop saw the BOGA had been sitting in their approval queue for 11 days (surfaced in the new campaign plan), they approved it within 48 hours. Hidden blockers stay blocked; visible blockers move. Every client plan now shows "Blocked-on-client" status + days-in-queue. (Goop BOGA, May 2026)
