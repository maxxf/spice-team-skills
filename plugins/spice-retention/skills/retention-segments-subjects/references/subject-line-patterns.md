# Subject line patterns that work

Pulled from Spice campaigns that hit benchmark or above. Mix and match.

## Direct value patterns

- "$X off your next [item]" — works when offer is the hook
- "Free [item] this weekend" — short, urgent
- "[X] points = [Y] reward" — math, no fluff
- "[Item] is back" — for returning menu items

## Curiosity patterns

- "Your points are about to expire" — urgency, no offer named (Ahipoki workhorse)
- "We saved your seat" — implies scarcity / personalization
- "About that order..." — followup signal
- "[X] minutes left" — timer language
- "One more thing" — conversational close

## Personalization patterns

- "[First name], we miss you" — warm winback
- "Hey [first name], that [item] you ordered..."
- "[First name], you're [X] points away"
- "[City] friends, this one's for you"

## What does NOT work

These were tested and underperformed in Spice campaigns or are known industry losers:

- "RE:" or "FWD:" fakes (open rate spike, unsubs spike worse)
- ALL CAPS subjects (deliverability hit)
- "Don't miss out!!" (high spam score)
- Emojis at the start (some inbox previews strip them)
- "Newsletter" or "Update" in subject (instant skip)
- "Free" + ALL CAPS + exclamation (triggers spam filters)

## Preview text patterns

The preview text is the "second subject line". Use it to extend, not duplicate.

- Extend: subject sets the hook, preview names the offer
  - Subject: "Your points are about to expire"
  - Preview: "Redeem for a free bowl by Friday at 11pm"

- Counter: subject sets urgency, preview softens
  - Subject: "Last chance"
  - Preview: "We still have a few hours left of the 20% off"

- Stack: subject + preview together form a sentence
  - Subject: "Big eats are back."
  - Preview: "And the new chicken shawarma bowl is killer."

## Voice rules

- Sentence case in subjects unless an All Caps word is intentional (e.g. "BIG" for MBFS, "VIP" for tier callouts)
- No emdashes ever
- No "Just" or "Quick" openers
- Use "you" / "your" — direct address beats third-person
- One verb minimum (preferred two)

## Bench data to compete against

Per Spice's actual recent campaigns:

| Client | Best subject 2026 | Open rate |
|---|---|---|
| MBFS | "Catering for the big game" | 47% |
| HealthNut | "Highlight Rewards: pick your $5 off" | 71% |
| Ahipoki | "Your free bowl expires Sunday" | 62% |

When generating new subjects, ask yourself: would this beat that benchmark for this client? If not, rewrite.
